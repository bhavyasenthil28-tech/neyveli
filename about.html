Neyveli – The Lignite City

Neyveli is a beautifully planned and lush green industrial township located in the Cuddalore district of Tamil Nadu, India. Famed across the nation as the "Lignite City," it serves as the central hub of operations for NLC India Limited (formerly Neyveli Lignite Corporation). Renowned for its vast open-cast lignite mines and massive thermal power stations, Neyveli is a major contributor to the country's energy grid and stands as a model of successful industrial urban planning.

Industrial Heritage & Growth

The modern history of Neyveli began with the discovery of rich lignite deposits in the early 1930s. What started as an agricultural landscape transformed rapidly following the incorporation of Neyveli Lignite Corporation (NLC) in 1956, inaugurated by India's first Prime Minister, Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru. Over the decades, Neyveli has pioneered open-cast mining technology in India, employing massive Bucket Wheel Excavators. The mined lignite fuels multiple local thermal power stations, generating thousands of megawatts of electricity that power Tamil Nadu and the southern states, solidifying Neyveli's status as a critical economic engine.

Neyveli Township & Development

Coinciding with the industrial growth, Neyveli Township was designed as a model grid-based residential layout. Divided into 32 distinct residential blocks, the township is celebrated for its abundant green canopy, parks, and wide, tree-lined avenues. It offers a self-sustaining ecosystem featuring top-tier educational institutions, well-equipped general hospitals, shopping complexes, recreational clubs, and sports facilities. The meticulous planning ensures that all residents enjoy easy access to clean civic services, local markets, and green open spaces, making it one of the most livable townships in the region.

Township Administration & Services

NLC India Limited

A prestigious 'Navratna' public sector enterprise under the Ministry of Coal, spearheading lignite mining, thermal power generation, and green energy projects.

Neyveli Township Administration

Manages civic amenities, housing, roads, water supply, sewage systems, and maintains the extensive parks and green forest cover within the township.

Health Services

Centered around the NLCIL General Hospital along with community clinics, providing top-class medical care, occupational health support, and wellness programs.

Education & Training

Boasts a network of high-performing schools, technical institutes, and training centers that have established Neyveli as a premier educational hub.

Nearby Places

Vadalur

Located just 9 km away, Vadalur is a spiritual town famous for the Sathya Gnana Sabha founded by the saint Ramalinga Swamigal (Vallalar).

Panruti

Situated 18 km north of Neyveli, Panruti is renowned as the jackfruit capital of Tamil Nadu and is a major trading center for cashew nuts.

Cuddalore

The district headquarters located 43 km away, Cuddalore is an ancient port city known for its historic temples and scenic coastal areas.

Chidambaram

Located 30 km south, this legendary temple town is famous for the historic Thillai Nataraja Temple and is a hub for classical art and learning.

Nearby Major Attractions

Vadalur Vallalar Temple

The sacred Sathya Gnana Sabha, built in an octagonal structure, where the focus of worship is the Jyothi (Eternal Flame of Truth), attracting lakhs of devotees during Thaipusam.

Chidambaram Nataraja Temple

A magnificent, ancient Dravidian temple dedicated to Lord Shiva as the cosmic dancer. It is famous for its golden roof, architectural marvels, and artistic significance.

Pichavaram Mangrove Forest

One of the world's largest mangrove ecosystems, located near Chidambaram, offering scenic boat rides through a complex network of waterways, canals, and rich biodiverse islands.

Silver Beach, Cuddalore

A pristine, sandy beach along the Bay of Bengal, famous for its long coastline, pleasant sea breezes, and recreational spots, making it a popular family destination.

Pondicherry

Located 60 km away, the former French colony is famous for its charming French Quarter streets, tranquil beaches, Auroville, and spiritual retreats like the Sri Aurobindo Ashram.
